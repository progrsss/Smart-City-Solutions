•Singapore•

Singapore is consistently ranked as one of the top smart cities globally, featuring a comprehensive high-speed telecommunication network with every part of the city integrated with smart technology, from advanced traffic control systems to sophisticated healthcare technology.


https://www.sganalytics.com/blog/smart-cities-in-the-world/

1. Real-time Traffic and Parking Data

Traffic Monitoring:

TrafficScan uses GPS data from taxis to calculate average traffic speeds and provide real-time travel information to motorists for route planning.

https://www.h2i.sg/singapore-deploys-smart-system-for-rainfall-monitoring-and-prediction/

OneMotoring traffic.smart provides an interactive map showing real-time road conditions, traffic alerts, ERP rates, and parking availability

https://www.h2i.sg/singapore-deploys-smart-system-for-rainfall-monitoring-and-prediction/

Singapore's Intelligent Transit System (ITS) provides a dynamic, real-time picture of population movement, with the Expressway Monitoring and Advisory System (EMAS) and guided parking system sharing data with motorists.

https://www.lta.gov.sg/content/ltagov/en/getting_around/driving_in
_singapore/intelligent_transport_systems.html

Smart Parking: IoT and sensor technology enable real-time data about parking availability and updates on traffic and road conditions through deployment of sensors and cameras.

https://www.directasia.com/blog/technologies-of-singapores-smart-car-park-system

2. Nationwide Digital Identity (SingPass)

Nationwide Digital Identity (SingPass) Singpass is a National Digital Identity that allows Singaporeans and residents to verify their identity securely when accessing services online, from registering births to accessing health records.

https://www.tech.gov.sg/products-and-services/for-citizens/digital-services/singpass

3. AI-Powered Flood Monitoring System

A smart rainfall prediction system developed by H2i and Furuno Singapore allows PUB (national water agency) to respond faster and more effectively to possible flash floods.

A rainfall monitoring and prediction system has been in place since 2018, providing early rainfall warnings and indicating where flash floods could potentially happen.

https://www.h2i.sg/singapore-deploys-smart-system-for-rainfall-monitoring-and-prediction/

https://thesourcemagazine.org/singapores-route-to-an-ai-enabled-future/

•United Arab Emirates-Dubai•

Dubai is recognized as one of the leading smart cities for innovative technologies focused on energy efficiency and sustainability.


https://energydigital.com/top10/top-10-smart-cities-2025

1.Blockchain Integration

•The Dubai Blockchain Strategy aims to move all applicable government transactions to blockchain, enhancing efficiency and significantly cutting down on resource consumption and service times.

•Dubai seeks to be the first blockchain-powered government using technologies like 5G communications, cloud and big data analytics, 3D printing, AI, IoT, and blockchain.

https://u.ae/en/about-the-uae/strategies-initiatives-and-awards/strategies-plans-and-visions/government-services-and-digital-transformation/dubai-blockchain-strategy

2.AI and Smart Service

•The DubaiNow app lets people complete over 120 different government tasks on their phones, meaning fewer trips to government offices and faster service.

•Rashid Chatbot is an AI-driven platform that assists residents with government-related inquiries, streamlining processes for starting businesses.

https://www.airtics.org/the-role-of-ai-in-dubais-smart-city-initiatives/
